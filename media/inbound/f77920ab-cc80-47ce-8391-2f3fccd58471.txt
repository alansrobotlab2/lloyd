## Part 1: The Idea

### Where It Came From

This started from Peter thinking about numbers as dimensions — not numbers *in* dimensions, but arithmetic operations *as* dimensions. The question was deceptively simple: what existed before there were any transformations?

The answer: you'd need the idea of a number. A single measurable unit. And then to get from 1 to 2, you'd need one of two concepts — addition or subtraction. Something gets bigger or smaller, more or less. That gives you a single axis.

But then multiplication enters the picture, and here's the key insight: **multiplication is just recursive addition**. "Multiply by 5" means "add this to itself 5 times." It's not a fundamentally new thing — it's the old thing applied to itself. And critically, it introduces **self-symmetry**: the operation of applying something to itself, which is one of the most primitive structural constraints that could ever exist. Anything truly primordial should be deeply symmetrical.

After that, exponentiation — which is recursive multiplication. The operation folding into itself again. A new plane of abstraction.

And then the question that cracked it open: **what would the third level look like?** And the realization that exponentiation *is* the third level. And the level after that — tetration — exists in mathematics but appears *nowhere* in fundamental physics. Three levels of arithmetic recursion might be all reality needs.

This connects directly to the Feigenbaum and edge-of-chaos work we've been doing in DFT, because we've been using those to track down the source of what prime numbers are. Peter's revised view: primes aren't the entropic seeds that mathematics formed around — they're the **smoothing edges** after collapse. The remains of the entropic seeds, but not the seeds themselves. After the multiplicative dimension has done its work, primes are what's left over — the irreducible residue at the boundary of multiplication.

**Arithmetic operations ARE dimensions.** Not metaphorically — structurally. Each operation is the unique recursive closure of the one below it, and each closure creates a new geometric degree of freedom with a specific symmetry type:

Three symmetry types — translation, scaling, rotation — are the generators of the **conformal group**, whose elements are exactly the **Möbius transformations**: f(z) = (az + b)/(cz + d).
